Pawns

The Pawn (♙, ♟) is the most numerous and weakest piece in the game of chess. Though it is weak, if left unattended, it can promote itself if it reaches the end of the board.

View details »

Knights

The Knight (♘, ♞) is a piece in the game of chess, represented by a horse's head and neck. Often compared to the Bishop in terms of value, the Knight may prove useful to trick enemies in tight spaces.

View details »

Bishops

The bishop (♗, ♝) is a piece in the game of chess that moves and captures along diagonals without jumping over intervening pieces. It acts as a long range attacker that pressures enemy pieces from a distance.

View details »

Rooks

The Rook (♖, ♜) is a piece in the game of chess that moves through any number of squares horizontally or vertically without jumping. Considered as a heavy piece, it is worth 5 points, 2 more than minor pieces such as the Bishop and Knight.

View details »

Queens

The Queen (♕, ♛) is the most powerful piece in the game of chess, able to move any number of squares vertically, horizontally or diagonally, combining the power of the Rook and Bishop. Powerful on your side and terrifying on the enemy's side, this piece is extremely valuable, second only to the King.

View details »

Kings

The King (♔, ♚) is the most important piece in the game of chess that can move to any adjoining square or perform a move known as castling. If a King is under attack and cannot escape, it is said to be checkmate resulting in a loss for the player.
